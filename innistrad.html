INNISTRAD




A Tale of Innistrads Design

More than all of everything that exists in reality, there is nothing. This vast nothing that cradles
life and existence is the Blind Eternities. Within the Blind Eternities, magic flows and pools.

Newly immortal, four young gods discovered a powerful intersection of magical energy. With
their budding power, they brought shape and order to the chaos.

Ubris - the first - created the vast and powerful Ocean designed after the Eternities themselves,
and it was everything.

Elhorrel - the second - fabricated the towering Mountains and vast continents with their
cavernous depths, and they rose above everything.

Solis - the third - shaped the vast Skies and space that locked the world together, and it
shrouded everything.

Instranova - the last - begot the flourishing forests and rolling fields of grass, the beasts of the
wood and the creatures of the deep, and they bloomed across everything.

✧

Though the gods were pleased with their creation, Instranova grew saddened. She besought her
brothers and sisters that they might join their power once more to grant the gift of knowing to
mortal kind.

Instranova saw the beauty of knowing. She knew that their creation would use it to treasure and
feel existence, that it would grow and change with them. She swore they may one day transcend
the gods themselves in creation.

Solis saw the value of knowing. She knew their creation would use it to foster understanding,
perpetuity, and connection. She swore to see them take form.

Elhorrel saw the potential of knowing. He knew their creation would desire power, prosperity,
and will. He swore to see them climb and master the world.

Ubris saw the folly of knowing. He knew their creation would use it to ruin and destroy, and
more than anything, he wished to defend their perfect world from cruelty, waste, greed, and war.
Ubris swore to protect and preserve his love.

✧

The triplet gods, angry and determined, devised a plot. The triplet gods promised a reunion.
The triplet gods lied.

They weaved the ancient ley lines together and bound the First. Ubris lost his power, and they
wrested his essence from his body and cast it into the Blind Eternities. His body became the
waves of the Sea.

When Ubris fell, they granted mortal kind the gift of knowing and celebrated the dawn of a new
age.

Elhorel fomented his design and brought himself before the newly realized mortals. He
demanded worship and gratitude for the gift. Solis and Instranova, horrified by Elhorrel's
command, overpowered and enfeebled him.

Defeated and rebuked, Elhorrel swore that if he could not dominate mortal kind, he would
destroy them. He descended to the heart of his creation and ignited his essence, bathing the
world's core in flames. His body became Elhelier— the fiery realm of the Demonlords.

His twisted creations began to raze and destroy their people; harbingers of the twilight of the new
age.

The twin gods wept and mourned, but Solis knew that mortal kind could not stand against the
Demonlords. On the brink of their extinction, she ascended into the distant skies and unfolded
her essence, bathing the world in light. The magic of the martyred god spawned the Soliscaelum
— the luminous realm of the Archangels.

The lone god Instranova chartered her sister's angelic creations toward the protection of their
people. The Archangel Sisters heralded the Age of Divine Conflict, the timeless struggle of
light against dark.

Alone and abandoned, Instranova used her power to read the magic of time. She saw mortal
kind splinter and divide. Heartbroken, Instranova brought herself before her creations and
shattered her essence, giving each of them a piece of divinity— and with it, a hope and a chance
for something different.

Instranova had fallen, and mortal kind honored her great sacrifice. They named themselves the
Innastri people and their world Innistrad after their savior.

The Archangels of the Soliscaelum and the Demonlords of Elhelier fight on still— but within
their people, the same beautiful affliction is shared.

The growth of struggle. The pain of sorrow. The blessed finality of death.


Geography

GAVONY -
Landmarked by the City of Thraben, Gavony has the largest population in Innistrad. It is patrolled by the Riders of Gavony, a sect of the Avacynian military, and is considered the safest of the four regions. While Hanweir is largely ungoverned, Thraben is managed and protected by the Church of Avacyn.

STENSIA -
Colloquially known as 'vampire land' since the rise of the vampire houses, Stensia has sparse civilizations and unpredictable weather patterns due to the Ashmouth. The Ashmouth, right at the center, is the largest known doorway to Elhelier, strictly guarded by the Gryffriders. Witches are rumored to practice in this region.

KESSIG -
Blanketed by the Ulvenwald forest, Kessig sprawls through the middle of Innistrad. Gastaff is at its heart, and to the very south, Avabruck is islanded. The two have tight trade relationships and are largely self sustaining, though the Avacynian Wilderangers keep protective tabs on them. The size of the Ulvenwald forest has shrunk considerably in the last twenty years, and after the loss of Trostad to Geralf, the mountain mines have been abandoned.

NEPHALIA -
With Havengull at its capital, Nephalia is a region of trade and industry sustained by the coast and the University of Drunau. It's home to Jenrick's tower, and further south, Rivstalt is run and operated by House Gallan. While the coast used to be heavily populated by ships and harbors, those have since all but disappeared after Innistrad's oceans became tumultuous. The ocean is now considered too dangerous to travel through and Havengull in particular has suffered from it.

Archangels

  • Archangel Liesa was the first angel of Innistrad. She is associated with contracts, shadow, balance, and fate. The least seen of the sisters, she also does not take any Chosen (as far as anyone knows). Her history is shrouded in mysters and her methods are unknown. She is rarely armed when seen.

  • Archangel Bruna is associated with knowledge, death, and perservation. Her chosen are scholars and historians, and much of her time is spent in Thraben, where she uses her knowledge of runes to keep the city magically protected.

  • Archangel Gisella is a warrior. Her Chosen are soldiers and combatants, and she ascends more humans to angelic status than any other Archangel. Single-minded and deadly, she can usually be found in the Avacynian military or areas of high conflict.

  • Sigarda is the Archangel of travelers. She's wild and unruly, usually handing our her magic freely to those who ask or inspore her. Her unpredictable nature makes her difficult to track down, but she still interacts with humans more than other Archangels. Buring the Green War between her and Demonlord Balthor, Lycanthropy was created.

  • Archangel Avacyn 700 years ago, Avacyn appeared and saved humanity from extinction. She then created the Church and aided in the establishing of Thraben. She is closely tied with the Lunarch and seems to be much more powerful than her elder sisters.
